FIDIC Notice Requirements Checklist

Red Book · Yellow Book · Silver Book

Every clause that gives you a right — to time, to money, to both — has a notice requirement attached to it. Miss the notice, lose the right. This is the reference sheet that stops that happening.

  • Six sections: EOT, variations, defects, payment, termination, general rules
  • Every deadline stated with its clause reference
  • Urgency-tagged: critical, high, standard
  • Field notes from projects where these went wrong
  • Checkboxes — built to be used, not filed

Daily Site Log

Contemporary record under Clause 20.1

Most daily logs record what happened. This one records what a claim needs — so when a delay event lands, the evidence already exists. It was written on an ordinary Tuesday, before anyone knew Tuesday mattered.

  • Fourteen sections covering the full working day
  • Idle-hour columns for labour and plant — the disruption claim lives there
  • A delay and access section flagged critical, tied to the notice checklist
  • Verbal instruction capture with written-confirmation tracking
  • Planned work for tomorrow, because planned-versus-actual is the whole argument

Risk Register

5×5 scoring · live heat map

The structure and the scoring engine, ready to use. The risks themselves are yours — they belong to your project, your contract, your site. What this gives you is somewhere disciplined to put them.

  • Three impact dimensions: cost, time, quality and HSE
  • Inherent score, mitigation, residual score — and the reduction between them
  • Live heat map and summary counting straight off the register
  • Dropdowns, conditional colour, filters, frozen panes
  • A contract-notice column that most registers do not have
